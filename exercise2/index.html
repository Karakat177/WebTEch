Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a theoretical physicist best known for developing the theory of relativity. He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.

Life and Legacy

Important Events

  1. 1879: Born in Ulm, Germany
  2. 1905: Published the special theory of relativity
  3. 1921: Won the Nobel Prize in Physics
  4. 1933: Moved to the United States
  5. 1955: Passed away in Princeton, New Jersey

Key Contributions

  • Theory of Relativity
  • Photoelectric Effect
  • Brownian Motion
  • E = mc² equation
